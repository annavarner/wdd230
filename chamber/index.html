News Release

Massillon City School Officials have announced that two new elementary schools will be built to replace the aging elementary schools in the district. The existing schools - Whittier, Franklin, and Gorrell are respectively 83, 67, and 66 years old. Besides their age, the current elementary schools are not equipped to handle new technology, are not energy efficient, and none have air conditioning. One of the new K-3 buildings will be built on the east side of the city, in the vistor parking lot at Paul Brown Tiger Stadium. The other will be behind the Massillon Intermediate and Junior High School building on the city's west side. The new elementary school campuses will improve safety, save on maintenance costs, and streamline transportation. Construction is set to begin sprint or summer of 2024 with the new schools opening for the start of the 2025-26 school year.
